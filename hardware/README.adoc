# Hardware Documentation

This gives some ideas about the hardware used, but without being overly precise. The way I built this first version is probably not optimal for producing it in numbers.
The electronic components to protect from flyback current or DC variations and oscillations are mounted on small pieces of stripboard (with 0.1 inch hole distance),
either 9 x 13 holes or 9 x 9 holes, and screwed to the housing using small M2 inserts, or M3 inserts where possible (also for the case).
                                                                                                                     
## Fire Safety

Instead of common PLA, the cases can be printed using filament of type SPECTRUM PETG FR V0 Natural, 1.75mm. This is self-extinguishing after 10 seconds,
rated UL 94 V-0 and appropriate for permanent installations.

For the cabling between the boxes, and the door strike, LAPP UNITRONIC® LiHH 2 x 0.25 mm² is a good choice. it is certified IEC 60332-1-2, IEC 61034-2
and halogen-free (LSZH), producing less smoke.
